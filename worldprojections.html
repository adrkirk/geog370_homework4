In this project I learned how to display images in different projections

To display maps in different projections, I first added a world map layer using the shapefile found in the "Worldprojections" Zip file. Next, I added Tissot's circles to the world map using the "Indicatrix Mapper" plugin in order to illustrate how various projections distort the area, shape, distance, and direction of geographic regions. Finally, I changed the map's coordinate reference system or projection through the EPSG button at the bottom righthand corner of the screen.

WGS84 Projection

Although distance, area and shape are slightly distorted, direction is preserved. This projection appears to achieve a favorable balance between distortion and preservation of these variables for navigation purposes.

Aitoff Projection

In this projection, shape, area, distance, and direction are all somewhat distorted. All of these characteristics are preserved best as you near the intersection between the equator and the prime meriidian.

Now, you should add the following projections on your own:

EPSG: 3857, 53018, 54034, 54027, 102016, and two additional projections that you choose.

WGS 84 / Pseudo-Mercator

The Pseudo-Mercator projection entirely preserves shape, but distorts size as you move farther from the equator. In result, a country like Greenland, for example, appears much larger than it actually is.

Sphere_Winkel_I

Similar to the Aitoff projection, the Sphere_Winkel_I distorts shape, area, distance, and direction, but achieves a favorable between all of these factors.

World_Cylindrical_Equal_Area

This projection severely distorts shape when you near each of the poles, but succeeds in preserving the area of countries and continents.

World_Equidistant_Conic

This projection preserves distance, but increasingly distorts area and shape as you move away from the North Pole.

North_Pole_Azimuthal_Equidistant

This projection preserves distance and direction while distorting area and shape.

World_Azimuthal Equidistant

In this projection, the equator is preserved, yet shape, area, distance and direction are all distorted.

World_Patterson

This projection preserves direction, but distorts size, shape and distance of geographic regions near the poles, exaggerating high latitude areas.
